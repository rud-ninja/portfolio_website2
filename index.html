Hello there! Welcome to my website.

I am an ex Software Engineer and Consultant with 3+ years of experience who chose to shift gears and chase his dream of studying Data Science. Now, they call me a Master in it. Why Data Science you may ask? Well, the answer is due to both philosophical and realistic reasons.
...
Like any other kid, I would find myself curious about the more difficult questions in life, such as, why does a certain thing happen the way it does or why do people behave in a certain way. The answers to which were never satisfactory. Studying science over the years has helped answer a lot of those questions, some of which I didn't even know could be asked! But the knowledge somehow felt second hand because I never got to dabble with all the concepts. It's like hearing about a movie from a friend's mouth versus seeing it yourself. So I wanted to have a first hand exploratory experience of everything.

But alas, when I went to the store to buy a potion of immortality to explore EVERYTHING, they said I had to do whatever I want in the time alotted to me. What a disappointment! But as luck would have it, I am born at a time when physical observation is not the only way to observe things, I can observe them through numbers and still have a satisfactory experience. And conversely, mathematical concepts such as the golden ratio and bifurcation theory do hide in plain sight as manifestations of natural phenomena. After this realisation and the fact that there was an actual scope to build a career out of it, began my quest for knowledge in data science.

The reason why I love data science so much is that it has an element of creativity as much as the wonder as to how one visualises and interprets the data. A project to me is like a vast landscape that I am free to explore and the best part is that the landscape may vary from time to time. Every facet of life generates data that is free to be explored if one craves to know about it. Although a bit of domain knowledge is always beneficial but as said before, these numerical observations are pseudo experiences of the phenomena and as the saying goes, we do learn from our experiences.

I created this website to display some of my projects in Data Science, feel free to browse through them. There are projects in Python and Power BI. If you are familiar with them, following along will be a cakewalk. For anyone who is new to the domain, I hope some of my projects will be able to create some interest in you regarding Data Science.
